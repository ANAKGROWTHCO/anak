How to Create Scroll-Stopping Ads

Posted on July 19, 2025 • by ANAK Team

In today’s fast-paced digital world, grabbing attention within the first 3 seconds is everything. Scroll-stopping ads are the secret weapon of successful brands they disrupt the feed, spark curiosity, and drive action.
But how do you create ads that actually make someone pause and click? Here are five essential elements to master.

1. Start with a Bold Hook

The first few seconds of your ad must instantly capture attention. Use bold headlines, shocking stats, or visually unexpected elements to make users stop scrolling. Think: “You’ve been doing this WRONG your whole life” or “Double your results with this one tweak.” Use curiosity or conflict to pull them in—and always tailor your hook to the platform and audience.

2. Use Eye-Catching Visuals

Whether it’s a bright color palette, crisp product shots, or high-motion video clips, your visuals need to pop. Use contrast and movement to your advantage, especially on platforms like Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok. Don’t use generic stock images people scroll past those subconsciously. Instead, create content that looks native but polished.

3. Keep It Short, Smart & Story-Driven

People don’t read long copy in ads unless it’s worth it. Use concise, punchy language that gets to the point fast. Structure your ad like a mini story—problem, solution, transformation. This emotional arc makes your message more memorable and persuasive. And if it’s a video ad, remember: captions are key for silent viewers.

4. Use a Clear CTA (Call-to-Action)

Every scroll-stopping ad must guide the viewer on what to do next. Should they shop now, sign up, download, or learn more? Make the CTA bold, obvious, and benefit-driven: “Get 30% Off Today” or “Try It Free for 7 Days.” The CTA should feel like a natural next step, not a hard sell.

5. Test Creatives Continuously

Even the best ad creatives can burn out quickly. The secret to consistently scroll-stopping ads? Testing. Run A/B tests with different visuals, hooks, and CTAs. Analyze which creatives perform best across different audiences and double down on what works. In 2025, agility in creative testing is as important as creativity itself.
